A Timeline of The Weeknd

Origins

- Born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Canada

- Raised in Scarborough.

Kin Kane

Before becoming The Weeknd, Abel Tesfaye performed under the stage name Kin Kane

- Kin Kane featured early music and performances in Toronto underground music scene

- Formed a duo with Jessy Ray called "Bulleez N Nerdz", followed by a mixtape release.

- Kin Kane experimented with his musical style and with multiple genres until he developed his now unique and iconic sound

The Weeknd

- Adopted the stage name "The Weeknd" in 2010

- Removed the "e" to avoid copyright issues with a Canadian band called "The Weekend"

- Became a part of The Noise, a production team

- Released three mixtapes in 2011: House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence

- Signed to Republic Records under his label "XO" in 2012

The Noise

- Formed a production team called "The Noise" with Kid Klassic.

- Tracks were leaked by producers that worked with The Weeknd via YouTube.

- The Noise disbanded in 2010

House of Balloons (2011)

Released: March 21, 2011

- First mixtape that gained widespread critical acclaim

- Known for "Wicked Games" and "House of Balloons/Glass Table Girls"

Thursday (2011)

Released: August 18, 2011

- Second mixtape of the Trilogy series

- Known for "The Zone" and "The Birds Pt. 1"

Echoes of Silence (2011)

Released: December 21, 2011

- Final mixtape of the Trilogy series

- Known for "D.D." and "Initiation"

Trilogy (2012)

Released: November 13, 2012

- Remastered compilation of the three mixtapes

- Included three additional songs

- Known for "Twenty Eight" and "Valerie"

Kiss Land (2013)

Released: September 10, 2013

- First studio album

- Known for "Adaptation" and "Live For"

Beauty Behind the Madness (2015)

Released: August 28, 2015

- Known For "Can't Feel My Face" and "The Hills"

- Won 2 Grammy Awards:

First #1 album on Billboard 200

First artist to hold top 3 spots on Billboard Hot R&B Songs chart

Starboy (2016)

Released: November 25, 2016

- Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album

Known for "Starboy" and "I Feel It Coming"

My Dear Melancholy, (2018)

Released: March 30, 2018

- Known for "Call Out My Name" and "I Was Never There"

- Received a Grammy Award for "Best Urban Contemporary Album"

- Debuted at #1 on Billboard 200

After Hours (2020)

Released: March 20, 2020

- Known for "Heartless", "Blinding Lights" and "Save Your Tears"

- "Blinding Lights", the second-released single of the album, is the #1 Billboard Hot 100 song of all time

Dawn FM (2022)

Released: January 7, 2022

- Debuted at #2 on Billboard 200

- Includes "Take My Breath" and "Sacrifice"

- Features Jim Carrey as radio host

Hurry Up Tomorrow (2025)

Release Date: January 24, 2025

- Upcoming studio album

- Known for "Dancing In The Flames", "São Paulo" (feat. Anitta)

- Release date was announced via Twitter, Instagram, Youtube & TikTok
